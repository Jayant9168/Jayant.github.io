🚨 Emergency & Critical Care Notes

8th Edition — ACLS Version | Quick Reference

💓 CPR — Universal Steps (Adult / Child / Infant)
1
Unresponsive? Not breathing or only gasping → Shout for help, send someone for AED/defibrillator
2
Check pulse within 10 seconds. If present → Give 1 breath every 5–6 sec; check pulse every 2 min
3
IF NO PULSE → Position supine on hard flat surface. Begin 30:2 compressions, push hard & fast ≥100/min, allow full chest recoil — minimise interruptions
4
Open airway: head-tilt / chin-lift, ventilate ×2 (avoid excessive ventilations)
5
Attach AED (adult + child >1 yr). Shockable?
YES → Shock ×1, resume CPR 2 min NO → CPR 2 min + ALS interventions

📡 Cardiac Arrest Rhythms
Coarse VF — Chaotic irregular activity → SHOCK
Fine VF — Low-amplitude irregular → SHOCK
Ventricular Tachycardia (VT) — Rapid wide complexes → SHOCK if pulseless
Asystole — Flat line, no activity → CPR only
PEA — Organised ECG but NO pulse → CPR only
📊 Common ECG Rhythms
✅ Normal Sinus RhythmRegular PQRST • PR 0.12–0.20s • QRS <0.12s • Rate 60–100
〰️ Atrial FibrillationIrregular rate • fibrillatory waves • no distinct P waves
⚡ SVTRapid narrow QRS • rate 150–250 • may have retrograde P
1° AV BlockPR interval >0.20 sec • all P waves conduct • benign
2° Mobitz I (Wenckebach)PR progressively lengthens → dropped QRS
⚠️ 2° Mobitz IIFixed PR, sudden dropped QRS — more dangerous
🚨 3° Complete Heart BlockP waves dissociated from QRS — requires pacing
Bundle Branch BlockWide QRS >0.12 sec • LBBB confounds AMI Dx
⚡ Cardiac Arrest Algorithm
Shout for help → CPR (30:2, ≥100/min) → O₂ → ECG → IV/IO
🔴 SHOCKABLE (VF/VT)
1
Defibrillate: 120–200J biphasic (360J mono)
2
Resume CPR ×2 min, start IV/IO
3
Epi 1mg IV/IO q3–5min OR Vasopressin 40U IV
4
Advanced airway → ventilate 8–10/min
5
3rd cycle: Amiodarone 300mg IV/IO (repeat 150mg)
🔵 NON-SHOCKABLE (Asystole/PEA)
1
CPR ×2 min, start IV/IO
2
Epi 1mg IV/IO q3–5min OR Vasopressin 40U IV
3
Advanced airway → 8–10 ventilations/min
4
PETCO₂ <15 → improve CPR quality
5
Treat reversible causes (Hs & Ts)
✅ ROSC: PETCO₂ ≥40 or pulse returnsVentilate 10–12/min • SaO₂ ≥94% • BP ≥90 mmHg • IV fluids • 12-lead ECG • Consider hypothermia if not following commands
⚡ Tachycardia — Unstable
Unstable signs: HR ≥150 • Chest pain • Dyspnoea • ↓LOC • ↓BP • Shock • Heart failure
→ Synchronised Cardioversion
Initial energy:
Narrow/Regular: 50–100J Narrow/Irregular: 120–200J Wide/Regular: 100J Wide/Irregular: Defibrillate
🐢 Bradycardia — Symptomatic
HR <60 with haemodynamic compromise
1st LineAtropine 0.5 mg IV, repeat q3–5min (max 3 mg)
2nd LineTranscutaneous pacing OR Dopamine 2–10 mcg/kg/min OR Epi 2–10 mcg/min
DefinitiveTransvenous pacing if refractory

M — Morphine
2–5 mg IV if pain not relieved by NTG

⛔ Fibrinolytic Contraindications (All must be "No")
❌ SBP >180–200 or DBP >100–110 mmHg
❌ Stroke >3h or <3 months
❌ Hx intracranial haemorrhage
❌ Head/facial trauma <3 weeks
❌ Major surgery/bleed <4 weeks
❌ Pregnancy
🥶 Hypothermia
Mild 34–36°CPassive rewarming — dry, blankets, warm room
Moderate 30–34°CActive external — forced-air warming devices
Severe <30°CCore rewarming: CPB, thoracic lavage, extracorporeal • Warm IV NS 43°C • Warm O₂ 42–46°C
If Arrest: CPR + defibrillate. Continue until rewarmed. Post-ROSC: rewarm to 32–34°C
🫁 PE & Trauma Arrest
PE Arrest: PEA is common. Standard ACLS + echo + consider fibrinolytic + consult for thrombectomy
1
Stabilise C-spine • Jaw thrust
2
Direct pressure for haemorrhage
3
Standard CPR + defibrillation + IV fluids
4
Consider resuscitative thoracotomy. Cricothyrotomy if airway impossible.

✅ Clinical Pearls
CPR Quality: Push hard ≥2 in, ≥100/min, full recoil, <10s interruptions. Rotate q2min.
Capnography: PETCO₂ <10 = poor CPR. Sudden rise ≥40 = ROSC. Target 35–40 post-arrest.
Defibrillation: Resume CPR immediately after shock — don't check pulse first.
🚨 Critical Reminders
"Time is muscle" — STEMI: PCI <90 min. Fibrinolytic <30 min.
Treat the PATIENT, not the ECG.
No hypothermic patient is dead until warm and dead.
