Definitions & Pathophysiology

Sepsis-3 Consensus
Sepsis

Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. Defined by an acute change in SOFA score ≥2 points consequent to infection.

Septic Shock

A subset of sepsis with circulatory and cellular/metabolic dysfunction associated with a higher risk of mortality. Clinically: persisting hypotension requiring vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg AND serum lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate volume resuscitation.

Pathophysiology Cascade

Clinical Pearl: Sepsis is NOT ruled in or out using a single biomarker. Diagnosis requires clinical evaluation of infection source + organ dysfunction (SOFA ≥2) — 2026 SSC Good Practice Statement.

The Hour-1 Bundle: Immediate Resuscitation

Critical Time
2026 SSC Update: For adults with septic shock, administer antimicrobial therapy immediately, ideally within 1 hour of recognition. For sepsis without shock: immediate antibiotics, ideally within 1 hour. For low-risk patients without shock: consider deferring while monitoring closely.
Lactate Management
  • Measure immediately at presentation
  • Remeasure within 6 hr if initial >2 mmol/L
  • Serial lactate measurements suggested to guide resuscitation
  • Capillary refill time (CRT) suggested as adjunct to lactate
Blood Cultures
  • Obtain prior to antimicrobial therapy
  • Minimum 2 sets (aerobic + anaerobic)
  • Do NOT delay antibiotics >45 min for cultures
  • Consider source-specific cultures (urine, CSF, wound)
Fluid Resuscitation Details
  • At least 30 mL/kg IV crystalloid in first 3 hours
  • Balanced crystalloids (LR, Plasmalyte) suggested over NS
  • Crystalloids alone suggested vs crystalloids + albumin
  • Frequent reassessment to avoid fluid overload

Mortality increases significantly with each hour of antibiotic delay in septic shock

Antimicrobial Stewardship Principles (2026): (1) Collect cultures before antibiotics when feasible. (2) Use local resistance patterns to guide empiric selection. (3) De-escalate to narrowest effective agent within 48–72 hr when susceptibilities return. (4) Use therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM) for selected patients. (5) Avoid Candida biomarkers to guide antifungal initiation/discontinuation.

Bundle Compliance & Clinical Outcomes

Data-Driven

Impact of Code Sepsis implementation on bundle compliance (n=118)

Sepsis-associated in-hospital mortality by region and severity

Hour-1 Compliance Impact
Hour-1 bundle compliance improved from 10% to 24% (p=0.03) after Code Sepsis protocol implementation. IV fluid bolus completion improved from 42% to 82% (p=0.001). Median hospital LOS reduced from 8.1 to 5.1 days (p=0.009).
SEP-1 Bundle Performance
Top reasons for bundle failure: (1) Fluid volume not administered 31%, (2) Repeat lactate not obtained 19.6%, (3) Antibiotics delayed >3 hr 16.5%. EHR-embedded sepsis tools achieved 98.6% 6-hour bundle pass rate vs 14.2% without tool use.
Global Mortality Disparity
High-income countries: 15–25% in-hospital mortality (sepsis), 30–40% (septic shock). Low/middle-income countries: 33.9% pooled case fatality. Every hour of antibiotic delay in septic shock increases mortality risk substantially.

2026 SSC Guidelines: 46 New Statements

Highlights
Screening & Recognition
Recommend performance improvement programs with sepsis screening, SOPs, and quality improvement strategies. Suggest "Code Sepsis" or "sepsis huddle" protocols. Suggest prehospital screening tools for acutely ill patients in transit.
Antibiotic Optimization
Recommend prolonged beta-lactam infusion after loading dose. Recommend de-escalation when microbiologic diagnosis confirmed. Suggest TDM on case-by-case basis. Suggest shorter antimicrobial duration with adequate source control.
Hemodynamics
Suggest MAP 60–65 mmHg for adults ≥65 years (vs higher targets). Suggest active fluid removal after acute resuscitation phase. Suggest capillary refill time to guide resuscitation. Balanced crystalloids suggested over 0.9% saline.
Adjunctive Therapies
Suggest IV corticosteroids for septic shock. Suggest against routine antipyretics to improve outcomes. Suggest against IV vitamin C, IV immunoglobulins, vitamin D, probiotics, beta-blockers, blood purification. Stress ulcer prophylaxis with PPIs suggested for GI bleed risk.
Respiratory Support
Suggest HFNC over conventional O2 for acute hypoxemic respiratory failure. Suggest HFNC over NIV as initial therapy. Suggest awake proning trial in non-intubated patients. Higher tidal volumes (6–8 mL/kg) acceptable without ARDS.
Source Control
Rapidly evaluate for anatomical source requiring emergent source control. Suggest against using Candida biomarkers to guide antifungal initiation. In low-resistance units, suggest selective digestive decontamination (controversial — ecological harm concern).
Post-Discharge Care
Suggest offering post-critical illness follow-up services. Suggest mental health support services after discharge. Good practice statement: comprehensive medication reconciliation at transitions. Health systems should implement sepsis survivorship strategies.
Pediatric Update
New data-driven pediatric sepsis definition. 20 new topics including POCUS guidance for resuscitation. 30–40% of pediatric sepsis survivors face long-term morbidity. Long-term follow-up now formally recommended.
Good Practice Statements (Not GRADEd): Sepsis is a clinical diagnosis not ruled in/out by a single biomarker. Blood cultures ASAP, ideally before antibiotics. Health systems should ensure patients and families have adequate information for hospital transition. Clinicians should discuss goals of care and prognosis with patients/families.
