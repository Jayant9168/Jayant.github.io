03Central Venous Access

💉

Internal Jugular Vein — CVC

Right IJV preferred · US guidance mandatory · Trendelenburg

High RiskUS GuidedSeldinger

Pre-Procedure Checklist

⚠️
Confirm INR <2.5, Platelets >50k. Obtain informed consent. Mark the site.
  • Verify indication & absence of contraindications
  • Obtain written informed consent
  • Review coagulation profile & platelet count
  • Confirm ultrasound machine available & functioning
  • Position: Trendelenburg 15°, head turned left
  • Apply cardiac monitor, pulse oximetry
  • Perform surgical hand scrub (chlorhexidine)
  • Don sterile gown, gloves, mask, cap
  • Prepare & drape sterile field (max barrier)
  • Flush all catheter lumens with normal saline

Step-by-Step Procedure

  • 01
    US IdentificationIdentify IJV in transverse — compressible, lateral to carotid
  • 02
    Skin Puncture18G needle at 45°, continuous aspiration of dark blood
  • 03
    Confirm Venous AccessUS visualize needle tip in vein lumen; free blood flow
  • 04
    Insert GuidewireJ-tip wire, advance 15–20 cm, monitor for dysrhythmia
  • 05
    Nick Skin & DilateScalpel nick, insert dilator over wire with twisting
  • 06
    Advance CatheterTriple-lumen to 15–17 cm (R) / 17–19 cm (L)
  • 07
    Confirm Blood ReturnAspirate from all lumens; flush and cap
  • 08
    Secure & DressSuture × 2 sites, apply occlusive dressing
  • 09
    Post-Procedure CXRConfirm tip at SVC/RA junction; rule out pneumothorax

Subclavian Vein — CVC

Infraclavicular · Lower infection risk · Higher pneumothorax risk

Pneumo RiskLow CLABSIInfraclavicular

Key Anatomical Landmarks

🚨
Highest pneumothorax risk (~3%). Avoid if ipsilateral lung pathology or severe coagulopathy.
  • Clavicle junction at medial/middle third (1 cm below)
  • Suprasternal notch as target landmark (medial)
  • Needle directed 15° inferior to clavicle plane
  • Keep needle parallel and pressed to clavicle underside
  • Turn head contralateral to reduce carotid risk

Step-by-Step

  • 01
    Position & PrepSupine, roll between scapulae, ipsilateral arm down
  • 02
    Puncture Point1 cm below clavicle at medial/middle third junction
  • 03
    Advance NeedleAim toward suprasternal notch, hugging clavicle
  • 04
    Blood AspirationNon-pulsatile, dark venous blood confirms vein
  • 05
    SeldingerGuidewire → dilator → catheter (17–19 cm)
  • 06
    Post-CXRMandatory — confirm placement, rule out pneumothorax

DVT RiskNo PneumoLandmark

Mnemonic: NAVEL

ℹ️
NAVEL (lat→med): Nerve · Artery · Vein · Empty space · Lymphatics. Target vein (1 cm medial to artery pulse).
  • 01
    PositionSupine, hip slightly abducted and externally rotated
  • 02
    LandmarkPalpate femoral artery pulse 2 cm below inguinal ligament
  • 03
    Needle Entry45° angle, medial to pulse; advance until blood aspiration
  • 04
    SeldingerInsert guidewire, dilate, advance catheter 20–25 cm
  • 05
    Confirm & SecureAspirate all lumens; suture; sterile dressing

    Post-ProcedureDocument; plan early removal or upper body conversion

04Rapid Sequence Intubation

🫁

RSI — Step-by-Step Protocol

Apneic oxygenation · Video laryngoscopy preferred · ETCO₂ mandatory

Critical SkillRSIETCO₂

Pre-Intubation (SOAP-ME)

✅
SOAP-ME: Suction · Oxygen · Airway Equipment · Pharmacy · Monitoring · End-tidal CO₂
  • Assess airway: LEMON score (Look, Evaluate 3-3-2, Mallampati, Obstruction, Neck)
  • Establish IV access × 2 (large bore preferred)
  • Attach full monitoring: SpO₂, ECG, BP, ETCO₂
  • Pre-oxygenate: NRB mask 15 L/min × 3 min (SpO₂ ≥95%)
  • Position: "Sniffing" or ramped (obese/pregnant)
  • Prepare: Video laryngoscope + direct backup
  • Prepare: ETT 7.0 cuffed, syringe 10 mL, stylet
  • Prepare: BVM + suction catheter (Yankauer)
  • Drug dosing calculated & drawn up
  • Rescue plan defined (surgical airway kit available)

Intubation Sequence

  • 01
    Induction AgentKetamine 1.5 mg/kg OR Etomidate 0.3 mg/kg IV
  • 02
    Cricoid PressureSellick manoeuvre at loss of consciousness
  • 03
    ParalyticSuccinylcholine 1.5 mg/kg OR Rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg IV
  • 04
    Apneic Oxygenation15 L/min nasal cannula; do NOT ventilate
  • 05
    LaryngoscopyLift at 45°, visualize cords — Grade I/II
  • 06
    Pass ETTCuff 2–3 cm past cords; remove stylet; inflate 5–10 mL
  • 07
    Confirm PlacementETCO₂ waveform + bilateral BS + CXR
  • 08
    Secure ETTTape or holder; recheck position
  • 09
    Post-IntubationPropofol/Midazolam + Fentanyl infusion
🚨
Failed Airway (3 attempts): Call help → SGA (iGel/LMA) → Surgical airway (cricothyrotomy)

06Emergency Procedures

⚡ Needle Decompression (Tension Pneumothorax)▼
🚨
Clinical signs: Hypoxia + Hypotension + Tracheal deviation + Absent BS + JVD. DO NOT wait for CXR.
  • 01
    Identify Site2nd ICS MCL or 4th/5th ICS mid-axillary line
  • 02
    Equipment14G (or 16G) angiocath ≥ 8 cm length
  • 03
    PunctureOver superior border of 3rd rib; remove stylet on air rush
  • 04
    Secure CatheterLeave in place; prepare for definitive chest tube
💓 Pericardiocentesis (Tamponade)▼
⚠️
Beck's Triad: Hypotension + Muffled heart sounds + JVD. Confirm with bedside echo.
  • 01
    PositionSemi-recumbent 45°, cardiac monitor, US-guided preferred
  • 02
    Subxiphoid Approach18G 10 cm needle; 1 cm below xiphoid, 45° toward left shoulder
  • 03
    Advance with AspirationContinuous aspiration; ST elevation = ventricular contact → withdraw
  • 04
    Aspirate FluidPericardial blood does NOT clot; ventricular blood clots rapidly
  • 05
    Consider DrainSeldinger to leave pigtail catheter for ongoing drainage
✂️ Surgical Cricothyrotomy (CICO)▼
🚨
CICO: Failed intubation × 3 + Failed SGA → Declare CICO → Surgical cricothyrotomy immediately.
  • 01
    LandmarkPalpate cricothyroid membrane midline between thyroid & cricoid
  • 02
    Horizontal Incision5 mm stab through skin AND CTM membrane
  • 03
    Dilate & InsertTracheal hook to hold open; pass 6.0 cuffed ETT
  • 04
    Inflate CuffVentilate, confirm ETCO₂ waveform
  • 05
    Secure TubeTie-down or suture; formal tracheostomy within 72h
⚡ Synchronized Cardioversion▼
  • Confirm unstable: BP <90, altered mentation, acute HF, ischaemia
  • IV/IO access established; oxygen running
  • Sedation: Midazolam 1–2 mg IV + Fentanyl 25–50 mcg IV
  • Select SYNC mode (confirm sync markers on R waves)
  • Energy: SVT/AFL 50–100 J; AF 120–200 J; VT 100–200 J biphasic
  • Apply gel pads; ensure no one touching patient
  • Deliver shock; reassess rhythm immediately
  • Document: Pre/post rhythm, energy, patient response
