Reperfusion Injury & No-Reflow
No-reflow phenomenon: TIMI flow 0–1 despite open artery. Rx: IC adenosine 100–200µg, Verapamil IC, GP IIb/IIIa inhibitors. Incidence 5–50% of pPCI.
Reperfusion arrhythmia: Accelerated idioventricular rhythm (AIVR) — benign, no treatment. VF in first 24h — defibrillation; not an ICD indication acutely.
Heart Block in STEMI
Inferior STEMI AV node block — usually transient; atropine / temporary pacing
Anterior STEMI Infranodal block — worse prognosis; urgent pacing
3rd degree AVB Transcutaneous pacing → transvenous if persistent >24h

PCI & Stent Selection
DES (Drug-Eluting Stent)
Preferred in all patients. 2nd gen DES: everolimus/zotarolimus-eluting. MACE reduction vs BMS. DAPT ×12 months minimum.
BVS/BRS (Bioresorbable)
Not routinely recommended (ESC 2023 IIb). Higher thrombosis risk. Consider only in large vessels ≥3.0mm.
Thrombus Aspiration
Not routinely recommended (TASTE, TOTAL trials). Consider bailout in massive thrombus burden TIMI 0–1.
FFR / iFR Assessment
Non-culprit vessel staging: FFR-guided PCI preferred over angiography-guided (COMPLETE trial — Class I 2023).
Special Populations
STEMI in Women: Higher presenting Killip class, older age. Atypical symptoms (dyspnoea, fatigue). Higher bleeding risk — weight-adjust anticoagulants. MINOCA more common.
Elderly (≥75 yr): Prasugrel relatively contraindicated. Prefer Ticagrelor or Clopidogrel. Dose-reduce with eGFR<30. Higher HF and mechanical complication risk.
Diabetic STEMI: Higher mortality, multivessel disease, worse myocardial recovery. Consider SGLT2i post-discharge (Class IIa). Insulin infusion if BGL>10 mmol/L.
MINOCA (MI with Non-Obstructive Coronary Arteries): 5–6% of STEMI. Rule out: myocarditis (CMR), Takotsubo, spontaneous coronary dissection (SCAD), coronary spasm. Specific treatment per underlying cause.
