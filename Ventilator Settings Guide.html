Section 01

Ventilator Modes

Selection and characteristics of mechanical ventilation modes for various clinical scenarios in adult critical care.

VC
Volume Control (VCV)
Mechanism: Delivers preset tidal volume regardless of airway pressure.

Settings: Vt, RR, PEEP, FiO2, flow rate, I:E ratio

Best for: Patients with intact respiratory drive who need guaranteed minute ventilation (e.g., neuromuscular disease, post-operative)

Guaranteed Volume Watch Pressures
SI
SIMV (Synchronized)
Mechanism: Delivers set number of mandatory breaths synchronized with patient effort; spontaneous breaths between.

Settings: Mandatory Vt or Pinsp, SIMV rate, pressure support for spont breaths

Best for: Weaning pathway, avoiding respiratory muscle atrophy

Weaning Mixed Mode
PC
Pressure Control (PCV)
Mechanism: Delivers preset inspiratory pressure; volume varies with compliance and resistance.

Settings: Pinsp above PEEP, RR, Ti, PEEP, FiO2

Best for: ARDS (protective lung strategy), poor compliance, bronchopleural fistula

Lung Protective Decelerating Flow
CP
CPAP
Mechanism: Continuous positive airway pressure with all breaths spontaneous.

Settings: CPAP level, FiO2

Best for: Non-intubated patients (OSA, cardiogenic pulmonary edema), weaning final step

Spontaneous No Backup
PS
Pressure Support (PSV)
Mechanism: Augments every spontaneous breath with preset pressure support above PEEP.

Settings: PS level, PEEP, FiO2, backup apnea ventilation

Best for: Weaning, spontaneous breathing trials, respiratory muscle conditioning

Comfortable Weaning
AP
APRV / BiLevel
Mechanism: Time-cycled switching between high and low CPAP levels; allows spontaneous breathing at both levels.

Settings: Phigh, Plow, Thigh, Tlow, FiO2

Best for: Severe ARDS, refractory hypoxemia, need for lung recruitment

Recruitment Expert Use
PR
PRVC / VC+
Mechanism: Volume-targeted, pressure-limited. Automatically adjusts pressure to deliver set Vt.

Settings: Target Vt, RR, PEEP, FiO2, max pressure limit

Best for: Variable compliance, need for guaranteed volume with pressure safety

Auto-adaptive Safe
NA
NAVA / Neurally Adjusted
Mechanism: Synchronizes assistance to diaphragmatic electrical activity (Edi).

Settings: NAVA level, PEEP, FiO2, backup ventilation

Best for: Patient-ventilator dyssynchrony, difficult weaning, pediatric patients

Section 03

Waveform Analysis

Recognition of pressure, volume, and flow scalars for patient-ventilator interaction assessment.

Pressure / Time
Square wave = volume control
Ramp/decay = pressure control
Volume / Time
Rising slope = inspiratory flow
Plateau = inspiratory hold
Flow / Time
Above baseline = inspiration
Below baseline = expiration
Patient-Ventilator Asynchrony Patterns
Double Triggering

Patient effort continues after ventilator cycles off. Fix: increase Vt, prolong Ti, or increase flow.

Flow Starvation

Patient wants more flow than provided. Fix: increase peak flow, change to decelerating pattern.

Ineffective Effort

Patient effort fails to trigger. Fix: adjust trigger sensitivity, reduce support/PEEP.

Reverse Triggering

Passive diaphragm contraction from vent inflation. Fix: adjust sedation, consider NAVA.

Auto-PEEP (Intrinsic PEEP) Detection
Signs:
  • Expiratory flow does not return to zero before next breath
  • Patient triggers immediately after expiration starts
Quantification: Expiratory hold; total PEEP - set PEEP = auto-PEEP.
Management: Increase expiratory time, decrease RR, bronchodilators, suction.
Section 04

Weaning & Liberation

Evidence-based protocols for spontaneous awakening, breathing trials, and extubation readiness.

✓
Daily Spontaneous Awakening Trial (SAT) Criteria
No active seizures, no alcohol withdrawal, no paralytics, no escalating vasopressors, PEEP ≤ 8 and FiO2 ≤ 0.5 (relative). Hold sedation, reassess q1-2h. Resume if distress, respiratory rate > 35, SaO2 < 88%, cardiac arrhythmia, or respiratory distress.
SB
Spontaneous Breathing Trial
Method: Low-level PS (5 cmH2O), CPAP (5 cmH2O), or T-piece for 30-120 minutes.

Pass Criteria (RSBI):
RSBI = RR / Vt (in L) < 105 predicts weaning success.
Vt > 5 mL/kg, RR < 35, stable SpO2, no distress signs.

Failure Signs:
RR > 35 or change > 50%, SaO2 < 90%, HR > 140 or change > 20%, SBP > 180 or < 90, anxiety/diaphoresis, paradoxical breathing.
EX
Extubation Readiness
Required: Resolution of underlying cause, airway reflexes intact, effective cough, manageable secretions.

Airway Assessment:
Cuff leak test > 110-120 mL (or qualitative leak around deflated cuff) suggests low post-extubation stridor risk.

High-Risk Factors for Failure:
Age > 65, cardiac failure, APACHE > 12 on extubation day, poor cough, excess secretions, MV > 7 days, upper airway pathology.

Section 05

Troubleshooting Guide

Systematic approach to common ventilator alarms and acute respiratory deterioration.

!
High Pressure Alarm
1. Patient Factors:
  • Bronchospasm, mucus plugging
  • Biting ETT, patient-ventilator asynchrony
  • Pneumothorax, pulmonary edema
2. Circuit Factors:
  • Kinks, water condensation
  • Filter blockage, closed HME
3. Action: Auscultate, suction, bronchodilate, check circuit, CXR if pneumothorax suspected.
!
Low Pressure / Disconnect
1. Circuit Factors:
  • Disconnect at Y-piece, ETT, or circuit
  • Cuff leak/deflation
  • Hole in circuit
2. Patient Factors:
  • ETT displacement
  • Bronchopleural fistula
3. Action: Check connections, inflate cuff, verify ETT position. If extubated, bag-valve mask.
O2
Hypoxemia Sudden Onset
Differential (DOPE):
  • Displaced tube — verify position
  • Obstruction — suction, check plug
  • Pneumothorax — needle decompression
  • Equipment failure — source gas
Action: FiO2 1.0, manual bag with PEEP.
C2
Hypercapnia / Acidosis
Causes:
  • Low minute ventilation
  • Increased dead space
  • Increased CO2 production
  • Rebreathing
Action: Increase RR or Vt, check auto-PEEP, treat fever/sepsis. Permissive hypercapnia acceptable if pH > 7.20.
