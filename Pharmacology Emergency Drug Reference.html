AntiarrhythmicCard 03
What is the amiodarone dose for refractory VF or pulseless VT?
Tap or hover to flip.
AmiodaroneDr. Mehta

Dose

300 mg IV/IO bolus, then 150 mg once if needed.

Used after defibrillation attempts in shock-refractory VF/pulseless VT; watch for hypotension and bradycardia.

TachycardiaCard 04
What is one important amiodarone contraindication or caution point?
Tap or hover to flip.
AmiodaroneDr. Mehta

Caution

Use caution in hepatic failure and avoid in second- or third-degree heart block without pacing support.

It can also prolong QT and worsen hypotension.

BradycardiaCard 05
What is the adult atropine dose for symptomatic bradycardia?
Tap or hover to flip.
AtropineDr. Mehta

Dose

1 mg IV every 3 to 5 minutes to a maximum total dose of 3 mg.

It is first-line for many unstable bradycardia cases, but may fail in high-grade AV block.

AtropineCard 06
Why should very small atropine doses be avoided?
Tap or hover to flip.
AtropineDr. Mehta

Warning

Doses lower than 0.5 mg may worsen bradycardia through paradoxical effects.

Also avoid relying on atropine alone in transplanted hearts or advanced conduction block.

SVTCard 07

Tap or hover to flip.
AdenosineDr. Mehta

Dose

6 mg rapid IV push, then 12 mg if needed, and another 12 mg if necessary.

Give through a proximal line with a rapid saline flush.

AdenosineCard 08

Tap or hover to flip.
AdenosineDr. Mehta

Contraindication

Avoid in irregular or polymorphic wide-complex tachycardia.

Use caution in asthma or reactive airway disease because bronchospasm can occur.

ShockCard 09
What dopamine infusion range is commonly used for bradycardia or shock support?
Tap or hover to flip.
DopamineDr. Mehta

Dose

5 to 20 mcg/kg/minute, titrated to clinical effect.

Used when atropine is ineffective and pacing is not immediately available, or for hypotensive states requiring vasoactive support.

PressorCard 10

Tap or hover to flip.
DopamineDr. Mehta

Caution

Dopamine can provoke tachyarrhythmias and increase myocardial oxygen demand.

Continuous ECG and blood pressure monitoring are important during infusion.

Indication

AntiarrhythmicCard 12
What is the lidocaine adult bolus dose in refractory VF or pulseless VT?
Tap or hover to flip.
LidocaineDr. Mehta

Dose

1 to 1.5 mg/kg IV, repeat every 5 to 10 minutes to a maximum of 3 mg/kg.

Lidocaine is generally considered when amiodarone is unavailable or unsuitable.

LidocaineCard 13
What toxicity clues can appear with excessive lidocaine exposure?
Tap or hover to flip.
LidocaineDr. Mehta

Adverse effects

Neurologic toxicity may include confusion, tremor, seizure, or decreased consciousness.

Cardiac toxicity can include bradycardia, hypotension, or worsening arrhythmia.

TachycardiaCard 20
What procainamide infusion approach is used for stable wide-complex tachycardia?
Tap or hover to flip.
ProcainamideDr. Mehta

Dose

20 to 50 mg/min until arrhythmia suppression, hypotension, QRS widening, or a total of 17 mg/kg.

Avoid if QT is prolonged or in heart failure with reduced tolerance to negative inotropy.

AV NodalCard 21

Tap or hover to flip.
DiltiazemDr. Mehta

Indication

Used for rate control in stable atrial fibrillation or atrial flutter with rapid ventricular response.

It is generally avoided in hypotension, decompensated heart failure, or pre-excited atrial fibrillation.

Dose

Rhythm strategyCard 28

Tap or hover to flip.
AvoidanceDr. Mehta

High risk setting

Avoid AV nodal blockers such as diltiazem in pre-excited atrial fibrillation or certain irregular wide-complex tachycardias.

They can accelerate conduction down an accessory pathway and worsen instability.
